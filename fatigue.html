01 Fatigue rule summary

Dashboard flags the most restrictive applicable client rule. Evidence and approvals are managed outside this dashboard.

Fatigue exposure is calculated from roster-derived worker metrics including rolling 21-day hours, current-month hours, consecutive shifts, consecutive day shifts, consecutive night shifts and days on site. Status is shown as breach, stand-down required, approval required or watch.

Client Core fatigue rules monitored Dashboard action
Fortescue Extended roster exposure; work beyond 14 continuous hours requires client authorisation; roster changes may trigger RCAT/FAID controls. Flags extended consecutive runs as watch or approval required.
Rio Tinto 243 h FIFO shutdown monthly limit; 14 consecutive FIFO shifts; standard 7 consecutive FIFO night shifts; up to 10 nights only under exception controls. Flags monthly hour breaches, excessive consecutive shifts, night-shift approvals and night-shift breaches.
BHP WAIO 230 h over 21 consecutive days; 14 consecutive FIFO shifts; 7 consecutive FIFO night shifts; minimum rest and sleep-opportunity controls sit outside current roster-only metrics. Flags 21-day hour breaches, excessive consecutive shifts and night-shift limits.
Roy Hill / Hancock 21 consecutive days maximum on site; mandatory stand-down after 14 consecutive day shifts; 24 h stand-down after 10 consecutive night shifts. Flags day-shift stand-downs, night-shift stand-downs and site-duration breaches.
