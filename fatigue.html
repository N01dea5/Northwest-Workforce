03 Hours compliance — 836h rolling 3-month limit

Flagged workers: any consecutive 3-month window ≥ 752h (close) or ≥ 836h (breach). Highlighted cells = worst window.

Every possible consecutive 3-month window within the reporting period is checked against the 836 h limit. Only workers whose worst window reaches the warning threshold (752 h, 90 % of limit) appear here. Highlighted cells mark the worst window for each worker; remaining months are dimmed. Data treatment: hours come from the DailyPersonnelSchedule at 12 h/shift for onsite, mobilising, demobilised, and confirmed rows. Rejected, declined, and late-withdrawal rows are excluded. The 13-day stand-down rule removes the 14th consecutive working day in any unbroken run, then resets the streak. Workers booked for every calendar day of a month are assumed to have 50 % actual on-site attendance and their hours are halved.

Limit: 836h per any rolling 3-month period  ·  Close ≥ 752h  ·  Breach ≥ 836h  ·  Stand-down applied: day 14+ in any consecutive working run is excluded.
